## 🇯🇵 JAPAN

### Visa Requirement
US passport holders **do NOT need a visa** for short-term tourist visits. Japan grants visa-free entry for stays of up to **90 days (approximately three months)**. No pre-registration or electronic travel authorization is required before arrival.

### Passport Validity
The passport must be valid for the **entire duration of the intended stay**. Japan does not require the common "6 months beyond stay" rule — validity matching your departure date is sufficient.

### Entry Forms / Arrival Cards
Japan has modernized its arrival process. **Electronic Customs Declaration Gates (e-Gates)** are available at major international airports, allowing paperless customs processing. Travelers may still complete a paper customs declaration card if carrying goods to declare (cash over limits, restricted items, etc.).

### Health / Vaccination Requirements
There are **no routine vaccination requirements** for entry into Japan for US citizens. No COVID-19 testing, vaccination proof, or health documentation is currently required. The US Embassy advises visiting the CDC page for the latest Japan-specific travel health information.

### Customs Rules
- **Cash limit**: Travelers carrying more than **¥1,000,000 JPY (approximately USD $10,000)** in cash or monetary instruments must declare it at customs.
- **Prohibited items**: Marijuana and all cannabis-derived products (including CBD oil) are **strictly illegal** in Japan. Many common US over-the-counter medications containing stimulants or pseudoephedrine are also **banned** (e.g., Sudafed, Vicks inhalers, certain ADHD medications like Adderall).
- Prescription medications containing amphetamines are **illegal to bring into Japan**, even with a valid US prescription.
- For permitted medications exceeding approved quantities, a **"Yunyu Kakunin-sho" (importation certificate)** must be obtained in advance from the Japanese Ministry of Health.

### Travel Advisory
**Level 1 – Exercise Normal Precautions** (US Department of State). Japan is considered one of the safest travel destinations for US citizens. Travelers are advised to enroll in the Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP) and be aware of the country's strict drug laws.

## 🇰🇷 SOUTH KOREA

### Visa Requirement
US passport holders **do NOT need a visa** for short-term tourist visits. South Korea grants visa-free entry for stays of up to **90 days**. However, the **Korea Electronic Travel Authorization (K-ETA)** system — which previously required pre-authorization — has been **suspended for US citizens** (the suspension has been extended through at least 2025), so no pre-travel electronic authorization is currently needed.

### Passport Validity
The passport must be valid for the **duration of the intended stay**. South Korea generally requires that the passport not expire before the traveler's planned departure date.

### Entry Forms / Arrival Cards
South Korea uses an **electronic customs and arrival declaration system**. Travelers complete arrival/customs declaration via the **Korea Customs Service app or paper forms** upon arrival. No separate physical arrival card is required for immigration, but customs declarations are mandatory for dutiable goods.

### Health / Vaccination Requirements
There are **no mandatory vaccination requirements** for US citizens entering South Korea for tourism. No COVID-19-related entry restrictions are currently in place. Standard CDC travel health recommendations apply (e.g., routine vaccines up to date).

### Customs Rules
- **Cash limit**: Travelers must declare amounts exceeding **USD $10,000** (or equivalent in foreign currencies) upon arrival or departure.
- **Prohibited items**: Narcotics, psychotropic substances, counterfeit goods, and certain agricultural products are banned or restricted.
- Prescription medications should be accompanied by a doctor's letter; some controlled substances require advance permission.
- Commercial quantities of goods may be subject to duty.

### Travel Advisory
**Level 1 – Exercise Normal Precautions** (US Department of State). South Korea is considered safe for US travelers. North Korea-related tensions exist but do not generally affect tourist areas in the south. Travelers are advised to stay informed of regional developments.

## 🇨🇳 CHINA (People's Republic of China)

### Visa Requirement
US passport holders **DO require a visa** to enter mainland China. A valid **PRC (People's Republic of China) visa** must be obtained before travel. Applications are made through the **Chinese Visa Application Service Center (CVASC)**. There is a limited **visa-free entry** policy in very specific circumstances (e.g., certain transit situations or newly announced short-stay exemptions under specific bilateral arrangements), but in general, advance visa application is required. Dual US-Chinese nationals are recognized **only as Chinese citizens** by Chinese authorities and must use their Chinese passport to enter.

### Passport Validity
The US passport must be valid at the time of entry, and generally should have at least **6 months of validity** beyond the intended stay, plus blank visa pages for the Chinese visa stamp.

### Entry Forms / Arrival Cards
Travelers must complete an **Arrival Card (Entry Card)** upon entering China, providing personal information, travel itinerary, and accommodation details. Customs declaration forms are also required. The **biographic information on the Chinese visa must exactly match the US passport**, including punctuation and abbreviations — any discrepancy can cause entry denial.

### Health / Vaccination Requirements
- **No routine vaccinations** are required for entry into China for US citizens.
- **Yellow fever vaccination** is required if arriving from a country with a **known yellow fever risk** (this rule applies even if transiting through an affected country for 12 hours or more).
- Vaccine requirements can change; travelers should consult **China's National Health Commission** and the CDC before departure.
- The US Embassy advises: **"Do not consume drugs in the PRC or prior to arriving in the PRC"** — a positive drug test (including for marijuana consumed legally in the US) can result in **immediate detention, fines, deportation, and a permanent ban from re-entry**.

### Customs Rules
- **Cash limit**: Travelers must declare cash amounts exceeding **USD $5,000** (or equivalent) upon entry. Foreign currency exceeding USD $10,000 must be declared.
- **Prohibited items**: Narcotics, arms and ammunition, counterfeit currency, politically sensitive materials, and certain publications are strictly prohibited.
- Certain medications require advance documentation; controlled substances are subject to strict enforcement.
- **Special permit required** for the Tibet Autonomous Region, in addition to a valid Chinese visa.

### Travel Advisory
**Level 2 – Exercise Increased Caution** (US Department of State, issued November 27, 2024). Key risks include:
- **Arbitrary enforcement of local laws**, including broad definitions of "national security" offenses.
- **Exit bans** — Chinese authorities have placed exit bans on US citizens, sometimes without notice, preventing them from leaving the country for extended periods.
- **Wrongful detention** of US citizens has occurred.
- Travelers are advised to avoid photographing protesters or police without permission, keep a low profile, and contact the US Embassy Beijing immediately if arrested.
- **US passports are NOT valid for travel to, in, or through North Korea (DPRK)** unless specially validated by the US Department of State.

There is a limited **visa-free entry** policy in very specific circumstances (e.g., certain transit situations or newly announced short-stay exemptions under specific bilateral arrangements), but in general, advance visa application is required. Dual US-Chinese nationals are recognized **only as Chinese citizens** by Chinese authorities and must use their Chinese passport to enter.

### Passport Validity
The US passport must be valid at the time of entry, and generally should have at least **6 months of validity** beyond the intended stay, plus blank visa pages for the Chinese visa stamp.

### Entry Forms / Arrival Cards
Travelers must complete an **Arrival Card (Entry Card)** upon entering China, providing personal information, travel itinerary, and accommodation details. Customs declaration forms are also required. The **biographic information on the Chinese visa must exactly match the US passport**, including punctuation and abbreviations — any discrepancy can cause entry denial.

### Health / Vaccination Requirements
- **No routine vaccinations** are required for entry into China for US citizens.
- **Yellow fever vaccination** is required if arriving from a country with a **known yellow fever risk** (this rule applies even if transiting through an affected country for 12 hours or more).
- Vaccine requirements can change; travelers should consult **China's National Health Commission** and the CDC before departure.
- The US Embassy advises: **"Do not consume drugs in the PRC or prior to arriving in the PRC"** — a positive drug test (including for marijuana consumed legally in the US) can result in **immediate detention, fines, deportation, and a permanent ban from re-entry**.

### Customs Rules
- **Cash limit**: Travelers must declare cash amounts exceeding **USD $5,000** (or equivalent) upon entry. Foreign currency exceeding USD $10,000 must be declared.
- **Prohibited items**: Narcotics, arms and ammunition, counterfeit currency, politically sensitive materials, and certain publications are strictly prohibited.
- Certain medications require advance documentation; controlled substances are subject to strict enforcement.
- **Special permit required** for the Tibet Autonomous Region, in addition to a valid Chinese visa.

### Travel Advisory
**Level 2 – Exercise Increased Caution** (US Department of State, issued November 27, 2024). Key risks include:
- **Arbitrary enforcement of local laws**, including broad definitions of "national security" offenses.
- **Exit bans** — Chinese authorities have placed exit bans on US citizens, sometimes without notice, preventing them from leaving the country for extended periods.
- **Wrongful detention** of US citizens has occurred.
- Travelers are advised to avoid photographing protesters or police without permission, keep a low profile, and contact the US Embassy Beijing immediately if arrested.
- **US passports are NOT valid for travel to, in, or through North Korea (DPRK)** unless specially validated by the US Department of State.
